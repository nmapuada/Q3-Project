Introduction

As it is March, the Fire Prevention Month, it is necessary to get to know all about fires. What causes them, what we could do to stop them, etc.
So, let us get started!

Fires are the effect of combustion, it is a chemical reaction. It happens when oxygen comes in contact with a fuel. It can be started using multiple materials, and oftentimes even by everyday objects.

How to Prevent Fires (Outdoors)

Putting out fires after using them

Leaving fires out is very dangerous. If you're not using it, you better put it out before you forget. The ash from the fire could set other things on fire, like surrounding foliage, belongings, people, etc. It's better safe than sorry.

You can put out fires using water, sand, or soil.



Stay a safe distance away from fires

Connected to the previous tip, staying a safe distance away from fire also helps. It would get really hot if you stayed too close to it, and you wouldn't want to inhale the ash. getting caught on fire wouldn't be nice either, would it?

Three feet away from the fire should be enough.



Always keep a fire extinguisher around

if you're going camping (or outdoors in general), you have to bring a survival kit with you. Fire extinguishers come in all sizes, not very sure about shapes though. If you will be near or handling a fire–if things go wrong–at least you're well-prepared with a fire extinguisher.




Mind your surroundings

Check whether your area has a lot of flammable objects near/around the fire–foliage, newspapers, twigs, etc. ZWhen these catch fire and go unnoticed, they could possibly burn down the surroundings, or catch them on fire. Be careful with the area you're in and thoroughly check it before setting up a fire.

Trim bushes, clean up litter, keep your belongings at a safe distance, etc.


Keep debris away from the fire

Again, connected to the previous tip! This is very important as fire spreads quickly, and that's how all forest fires/wildfires start. They all somehow stem from neglect, which is pretty sad, but it's not like you could move the trees away.

What you can do here is to clean up anything that could catch on fire and spread out into your surroundings, make sure your fire is controlled and in a safe area, and not to put anything flammable close to the fire.
